Basic English Grammar

1. Simple Present Tense

Explanation : Used to express habits, facts, or general truths.

GENERAL FORMULA:

  • I/You/We/They + Verb 1
  • He/She/It + Verb 1 + s/es

Active Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: S + V1 + O ‚Üí She reads a book every day.
  • Negative: S + do/does + not + V1 + O ‚Üí She does not read a book every day.
  • Interrogative: Do/Does + S + V1 + O? ‚Üí Does she read a book?

Passive Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: O + is/am/are + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book is read by her.
  • Negative: O + is/am/are + not + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book is not read by her.
  • Interrogative: Is/Am/Are + O + V3 (+ by + S)? ‚Üí Is a book read by her?

NOTE : Verb 1 means the basic form of the verb (without -ing, -ed, or to).
Example of Verb 1: read, write, eat, go, play

2. Simple Past Tense

Explanation : Used to express events or activities that have occurred in the past, usually accompanied by time information such as yesterday, last night, two days ago, in 2000, etc.

GENERAL FORMULA:

  • Semua subjek (I, You, We, They, He, She, It) + Verb 2 (V2)

Active Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: S + V2 + O ‚Üí She read a book yesterday.
  • Negative: S + did not + V1 + O ‚Üí She did not read a book yesterday.
  • Interrogative: Did + S + V1 + O? ‚Üí Did she read a book yesterday?

Passive Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: O + was/were + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book was read by her yesterday.
  • Negative: O + was/were + not + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book was not read by her yesterday.
  • Interrogative: Was/Were + O + V3 (+ by + S)? ‚Üí Was a book read by her yesterday?

NOTE : Verb 2 (V2) is the past form of the verb
Example of Verb 2: go ‚Üí went, eat ‚Üí ate, read ‚Üí read (read as ‚Äúred‚Äù)

3. Simple Future Tense

Explanation: Used to express something that will happen in the future.

GENERAL FORMULA

  • S + will + V1

Active Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: S + will + V1 + O ‚Üí She will read a book tomorrow.
  • Negative: S + will not + V1 + O ‚Üí She will not read a book tomorrow.
  • Interrogative: Will + S + V1 + O? ‚Üí Will she read a book tomorrow?

Passive Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: O + will be + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book will be read by her tomorrow.
  • Negative: O + will not be + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book will not be read by her tomorrow.
  • Interrogative: Will + O + be + V3 (+ by + S)? ‚Üí Will a book be read by her tomorrow?

NOTE : The verb after ‚Äúwill‚Äù is always in the base form (V1), not V2 or V3.
Example: will go, will eat, will read.

4. Present Continuous Tense

Explanation: Used to express an action that is currently taking place.

GENERAL FORMULA:

  • S + am/is/are + V-ing

Active Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: S + am/is/are + V-ing + O ‚Üí She is reading a book now.
  • Negative: S + am/is/are + not + V-ing + O ‚Üí She is not reading a book now.
  • Interrogative: Am/Is/Are + S + V-ing + O? ‚Üí Is she reading a book now?

Passive Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: O + am/is/are + being + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book is being read by her now.
  • Negative: O + am/is/are + not + being + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí A book is not being read by her now.
  • Interrogative: Am/Is/Are + O + being + V3 (+ by + S)? ‚Üí Is a book being read by her now?

NOTE : Verbs in the -ing form (V-ing) always follow am/is/are to indicate ongoing activity.

5. Present Perfect Tense

Explanation : Used to express actions that happened in the past but are still relevant now, to talk about life experiences without mentioning the exact time, or to show the result of an action.

GENERAL FORMULA:

  • S + have/has + V3 (past participle)

Active Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: S + have/has + V3 + O ‚Üí She has finished her homework.
  • Negative: S + have/has + not + V3 + O ‚Üí She has not finished her homework.
  • Interrogative: Have/Has + S + V3 + O? ‚Üí Has she finished her homework?

Passive Voice Formula and Examples:

  • Positive: O + have/has + been + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí The homework has been finished by her.
  • Negative: O + have/has + not + been + V3 (+ by + S) ‚Üí The homework has not been finished by her.
  • Interrogative: Have/Has + O + been + V3 (+ by + S)? ‚Üí Has the homework been finished by her?

NOTE : ‚ÄúHave‚Äù is used with the subjects I, You, We, They, while ‚ÄúHas‚Äù is used with He, She, It.

6. Conditional Tenses

Explanation: Conditional tenses are used to express conditions or situations that affect a certain result or consequence. Usually, conditional sentences have two parts:

  • Zero Conditional (General facts / habits)

    • Explanation: Used to express general truths, habits, or rules that are always true if the condition is met.

    formula : If + Present Simple, Present Simple ‚Üí If you heat water, it boils.

  • First Conditional (Real possibility in the future)

    • Explanation: Used to express a real or very likely possibility in the future if the condition is fulfilled.

    formula : If + Present Simple, will + V1 ‚Üí If it rains tomorrow, we will cancel the trip.

  • Second Conditional (Unreal or hypothetical present/future)

    • Explanation: Used to talk about unreal or imaginary situations in the present or future.

    formula : If + Past Simple, would + V1 ‚Üí If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world.

  • Third Conditional (Unreal past situation)

    • Explanation: Used to talk about situations that did not happen in the past and their imagined results.

    formula : If + Past Perfect, would have + V3 ‚Üí If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

NOTE : In conditional sentences, the order of clauses can be reversed without changing the meaning, but if the if-clause comes first, it‚Äôs usually followed by a comma.
Example: If it rains, we will stay home. = We will stay home if it rains.

7. Relative Pronouns

Explanation: Relative pronouns are pronouns used to connect relative clauses (explanatory sentences) with the main clause. Relative pronouns function to provide additional information about people, objects, animals, or places that have been mentioned previously in the sentence.

  • Who

    • Explanation: Refers to people (subject of the relative clause)

    formula : Noun (person) + who + Verb ‚Üí The man who called you is waiting.

    • Whom

      • Explanation: Refers to people (object of the relative clause)

      formula : Noun (person) + whom + Subject + Verb ‚Üí The woman whom I met yesterday is a doctor.

    • Whose

      • Explanation: Shows possession (for people or things).

      formula : Noun + whose + Noun ‚Üí The student whose phone rang was embarrassed.

    • Which

      • Explanation: Refers to things or animals (subject or object)

      formula : Noun (thing/animal) + which + Verb ‚Üí The book which is on the table is mine.

    • Where

      • Explanation: Refers to places

      formula : Place + where + Verb ‚Üí This is the house where I grew up.

    NOTE : Who is for subjects (people), whom is for objects (people). In spoken English, whom is often replaced by who.

    8. Subject-Verb Agreement

    Explanation:Subject-Verb Agreement means that the verb in a sentence must match the subject in number (singular or plural).

    formula :

    • Singular: Subject + Verb-s/es
    • Plural: Subject + Verb (base form)

    Examples:

    • She likes coffee.
    • They like coffee.
    • I eat breakfast at 7.
    • He eats breakfast at 7.

    Remember this:

    • I / You / We / They ‚Üí use the base verb (no -s or -es)
    • He / She / It ‚Üí use the verb + -s / -es
    9. Active & Passive Participle

    Explanation : A participle is a form of a verb used as an adjective or to form verb tenses. There are two main types:

    1. Active Participle (Present Participle)

      Function: Shows that the noun is doing the action.
      Form: Verb + ing
      Use: Describes an active role.

    Example : The running man won the race.
    (The man is doing the action: running)

    2. Passive Participle (Past Participle)

      Function: Shows that the noun is receiving the action.
      Form: Verb 3 (Past Participle)
      Use: Describes a passive role.

    Example : The broken window needs repair.
    (The window was broken by someone)

    Basic Conversations

    1. Introducing Yourself

    Anna: Hello! My name is Anna. What's your name?

    Budi: Hi Anna! I'm Budi. Nice to meet you.

    Anna: Nice to meet you too, Budi. Where are you from?

    Budi: I'm from Indonesia. How about you?

    Anna: I'm from the United States. Are you a student?

    Budi: Yes, I am. I'm studying English. What about you?

    Anna: I'm a teacher. I teach English.

    Budi: That's great! Maybe you can help me practice my English.

    Anna: Of course! I'd be happy to help.

    Key Vocabulary:
    • What's your name? ‚Äì Siapa nama kamu?
    • Nice to meet you ‚Äì Senang bertemu denganmu
    • Where are you from? ‚Äì Kamu berasal dari mana?
    • I'm from... ‚Äì Saya berasal dari...
    • Are you a student? ‚Äì Apakah kamu seorang pelajar?
    • I'm studying... ‚Äì Saya sedang belajar...
    • I teach... ‚Äì Saya mengajar...

    2. Ordering Food at a Restaurant

    Waiter: Hello! Welcome to our restaurant. Here's the menu.

    Customer: Thank you. Can I have a chicken sandwich, please?

    Waiter: Sure. Would you like anything to drink?

    Customer: Yes, I'd like an orange juice, please.

    Waiter: One chicken sandwich and one orange juice. Anything else?

    Customer: No, that's all for now. Thank you.

    Waiter: You're welcome. Your food will be ready in about 10 minutes.

    Key Vocabulary:
    • Welcome to... ‚Äì Selamat datang di...
    • Can I have...? ‚Äì Bolehkah saya pesan...?
    • Would you like...? ‚Äì Apakah Anda ingin...?
    • I'd like... ‚Äì Saya ingin...
    • Anything else? ‚Äì Ada yang lain?
    • That's all ‚Äì Itu saja
    • You're welcome ‚Äì Sama-sama
